What is Manahyme Kyokupara?

Manahyme Kyokupara is an upcoming free-to-play (or rather, TRULY free-to-win) rhythm game you would expect; just some rehash of "insert name of rhythm game here", et cetera, but in truth more than just one of them!

Gameplay Logic
  • Basic gameplay will be like osu!, with a few modifications to its types of notes.
  • However, the song clear condition will use beatmania IIDX's Groove Guage (Fill up the guage to a certain threshold by the end of the song to clear, but a single note break drops the guage by a certain amount).
  • The mapping style will be initially osu!-style with a sprinkle of Taiko No Tatsujin-esque note distribution.
  • Two scoring systems are used; the osu! scoring system (as the raw score), and beatmania IIDX's EX score system (as the rank score).
  • Difficulty calculation system will initally be ranged from zero (0) to twelve (12) units. Unit name is currently not finalized.
  • Gameplay mod system will initially be comprised of:
    • Assistance: Easy Diff (decreases overall difficulty of the song), Slow Motion (decreases song BPM to 0.5x speed), Super Slow Motion (extension of Slow Motion; decreases song BPM to 0.33x speed), Loose Judge (increases timing window of notes) and Failsafe (treat falied songs as clears)
    • Intensity: Hard Rock (increases overall difficulty of the song), Sudden Death (break the combo and you instantly fail the song), Perfection (extension of Sudden Death; player must pass with 100% accuracy), Sonic (increases song BPM to 1.5x speed), Super Sonic (extension of Sonic; increases song BPM to 2x speed), Fadeout (notes fade out before hit), Hidden (extension of Fadeout; note will not appear at all) and Strict Judge (decrease timing window of notes)
    • Fun: Relaxation (player cursor moves automatically), Automatic (autoplay), Cinematic (view only storyboard or video)
User Meta
  • Overall User Play Progress will use an adventure-like system
  • Overall User Play Ranking will use a modified version of osu!'s PP system
  • Account-wide personalization system (complete with signature skin, titles, etc.)
  • User Hierachy System (to determine whether user is normal player, dev, mod, etc.)
Song Meta
  • Called beatmaps like osu!
  • Difficulties named in ascending order of intensity: Novice, Intermediate, Advanced, Extreme, Master, Challenge (latter difficulty can allow large gap unlike other difficulties)
  • Can be favorited or (unlike osu!) blacklisted by user
  • Live Genre Classification System (other users can change genres of beatmaps, not limited to beatmap creator)
  • Beatmap Autorization System (akin to osu!'s beatmap ranking system)
  • Beatmap Song Live Identification System (to keep track of the same songs that are offered)
  • Copyright Metadata (to ensure disclaimer of ownership of songs)
  • Explicit Content Marking (to identify songs that are considered offensive to some players, linked to Beatmap Song Live Identification System)
